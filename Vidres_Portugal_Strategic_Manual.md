## 1. Portuguese Ceramic Raw Materials Market

### 1.1 Structure of the demand side

Portuguese ceramics splits into three buyer segments, each with different raw-material (*matéria-prima*) economics:

| Segment | Examples | What they buy | Margin sensitivity |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Premium tableware / loiça** (porcelain & fine earthenware) | Vista Alegre, Costa Verde, Spal | High-whiteness fritas, lead-free glazes, custom decoration glazes, on-glaze/in-glaze colours | **Quality > price.** Batch consistency dominates. |
| **Mass tableware & stoneware** | Matceramica, Grestel, regional producers | Standard glossy/matte glazes, engobes, base frits | **Price + energy** sensitive, but reject defects. |
| **Sanitaryware & tiles** | Sanindusa, Love Tiles, Revigrés, Margres, Aleluia | Engobes, glazes, frits, digital inks (tiles) | Volume-driven, energy-driven. |

> **Strategic focus for Vidres Portugal:** lead with **premium loiça (Vista Alegre / Costa Verde)** as reference accounts — winning them is a *quality certificate* that opens every other door — then expand into stoneware and sanitaryware where energy savings convert fastest into orders.

### 1.2 Geography — the Aveiro / Centro cluster

The Portuguese ceramic industry is geographically concentrated, which is a **structural advantage for a local supplier**:

- **Aveiro / Ílhavo / Vagos** — porcelain & tableware (Vista Alegre, Costa Verde, Grestel).
- **Alcobaça / Caldas da Rainha / Leiria** — decorative and traditional loiça.
- **Aveiro–Águeda corridor** — sanitaryware and tiles.

A local technical hub (lab + warehouse + applications engineers) placed in the Aveiro region puts Vidres **within 60–90 minutes** of most strategic accounts. Torrecid and Esmalglass ship and fly in from Castellón, Spain (≈ 900 km / a full day of logistics). **Proximity is the single most defensible moat we have.**

### 1.3 Market dynamics shaping demand (2024–2027)

- **Energy cost shock** — gas + electricity remain the #1 P&L line item for kilns; any firing-energy reduction is bankable.
- **Sustainability regulation & branding** — EU CBAM, customer ESG demands, and "Made in Portugal" premium branding push for lower-carbon, lead-free, locally-sourced inputs.
- **Reshoring & premiumization** — European tableware brands are defending margin through design and sustainability, not low cost — they *want* a technical innovation partner.
- **Skills concentration** — fewer in-house glaze chemists at factories → demand for supplier-side technical service is rising.

> **Takeaway:** the market is shifting from "sell me cheap frit" to "help me fire cheaper, cleaner, and more consistently." That is exactly the gap an agile, local, technical Vidres can own.

## 3. Pain Points of Portuguese Manufacturers

Factories like Vista Alegre and Costa Verde do **not** buy "raw material." They buy the elimination of four pains. These are our entire value proposition.

### Pain 1 — Batch-to-batch consistency *(the #1 churn driver)*

If a frit behaves differently today vs. two months from now —
- tone shifts, melting behavior changes, gloss changes, firing defects appear —
**the customer leaves for Torrecid.** This is the critical KPI.

**Vidres answer:**
- **Statistical Process Control (SPC)** on every batch with a published **Certificate of Analysis** (chemistry + key physical properties).
- **Spectrophotometric ΔE locking** — guarantee **ΔE ≤ 0.5** between batches on reference standards.
- **Retained reference samples** + traceability for every lot, queryable by the customer.
- **Consistency SLA** written into the contract — a commercial guarantee, not a marketing claim.

### Pain 2 — Energy efficiency *(the #1 P&L pressure)*

Portuguese plants are squeezed hard on gas and electricity. They want:
- **lower firing temperature**, **shorter firing cycle**, **lower energy consumption** per piece.

**Vidres answer:** the **Eco-Low Fire Smart Frit System** (see §5) — explicitly engineered to cut firing temperature 30–50 °C while preserving whiteness and gloss → **8–12% kiln energy reduction**.

### Pain 3 — Slow custom development *(Torrecid's lab is their fortress)*

Factories need fast turnaround on custom fritas, specialty glazes, and texture/reactive effects.

**Vidres answer:**
- A **local applications lab** in the Aveiro cluster.
- **Digital color matching** + a standardized base-frit architecture (see §4) → sample in **≤ 10 days** instead of 4–6 weeks.
- A named project owner per account; weekly development cadence.

### Pain 4 — Onsite technical support *(proximity beats Spain)*

Portuguese factories value closeness. An engineer who shows up and fixes a production-line problem in **24–48h** is a decisive advantage over Castellón-based competitors.

**Vidres answer:**
- **Field Applications Engineers** based in Portugal, contractually committed to **24–48h onsite response**.
- Joint troubleshooting protocol (defect → root cause → corrective batch) co-owned with the customer's process team.

> **Slide line:** "We don't sell frit. We sell **consistency, lower energy bills, faster development, and an engineer at your line tomorrow.**"

## 4. Cost Optimization Strategy

Three structural levers let Vidres protect margin *while* underpricing rigid competitors on TCO.

### A) Local Iberian sourcing — silica & recycled cullet

Source silica, feldspar, and **recycled cullet** from Portuguese/Iberian suppliers, and recover **glass cullet from the customers' own reject streams**.

- ↓ **transport cost** (no cross-Iberia freight)
- ↓ **lead time** (days, not weeks)
- ↓ **CO₂ footprint** (sustainability story + CBAM hedge)
- ↑ **circular-economy narrative** — "we recycle your factory's own cullet back into your glaze."

### B) Standardized base-frit architecture *(the biggest R&D saver)*

Do **not** maintain 50 bespoke recipes. Build:
- **5 base frit systems** (e.g., glossy transparent, white opaque, matte, low-fire eco, reactive/texture base),
- then **customize through additives, colorants, and engobe layering** on top of the 5 bases.

Benefits:
- collapses R&D and QC complexity,
- raw-material **purchasing leverage** (buy few inputs in volume),
- faster custom turnaround (start from a proven base, not a blank page),
- far easier batch consistency (fewer formulations to control).

```
        ┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
        │            5 STANDARD BASE FRITS          │
        │  Glossy · White-Opaque · Matte ·          │
        │  Eco-Low-Fire · Reactive/Texture          │
        └──────────────────────────────────────────┘
                          │  + additives / colorants / engobes
        ┌─────────────────┼─────────────────┐
        ▼                 ▼                 ▼
   Vista Alegre      Costa Verde        Stoneware
   custom white      custom gloss       custom matte
```

### C) Digital color matching & recipe automation

Automate formulation with a spectro-driven recipe engine.
- ↓ **waste** (fewer scrapped trials)
- ↓ **test cycles** (right-first-time formulation)
- ↓ **development time** (and therefore cost-to-serve per custom job)

**Combined effect:** A+B+C reduce **cost-to-serve and R&D cost by an estimated 20–35%**, funding both competitive pricing *and* the free technical service that locks in accounts.

## 5. Innovation Roadmap

Two flagship innovations move Vidres from "supplier" to "technology partner."

### 5.1 Flagship #1 — **Eco-Low Fire Smart Frit System** *(strongest commercial weapon)*

A frit/glaze line engineered to:
- **lower firing temperature by 30–50 °C**,
- **shorten the firing cycle**,
- **reduce energy consumption**,
- while **preserving whiteness and gloss** (non-negotiable for premium loiça).

**Why it wins in Portugal:** customers are paying enormous energy bills. The pitch
> **"8–12% saving on kiln energy"**
is a *stronger argument than price*, because it recurs every single firing, forever.

**Quantified value example (illustrative, to be validated in pilot):**

| Metric | Baseline | With Eco-Low-Fire | Benefit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Peak firing temp | 1180 °C | 1130–1150 °C | −30 to −50 °C |
| Kiln energy/piece | index 100 | 88–92 | **−8 to −12%** |
| Cycle time | index 100 | 90–95 | faster throughput |
| Whiteness / gloss | reference | maintained | no quality trade-off |

> Convert the % into **€/year on the customer's actual gas bill** in every proposal. That single number closes deals.

### 5.2 Flagship #2 — **AI Glaze Prediction Platform** *(tech-supplier positioning)*

The customer inputs:
- **body composition**, **kiln curve**, **desired finish (gloss/matte/whiteness/texture)**,

and the platform **recommends a starting recipe** (from the 5 base systems + additive set), predicts firing behavior, and flags defect risks.

- Almost **no local competitor offers this.**
- Repositions Vidres as a **tech partner, not a raw-material plant.**
- Creates a **data moat**: every pilot and batch feeds the model → predictions get better → switching cost for the customer rises.
- Pairs naturally with the **digital color matching** engine from §4C (shared data backbone).

### 5.3 Roadmap sequencing

```
 H1 (M1–M6)   ▶ 5 base-frit standardization + SPC/ΔE consistency system
 H2 (M4–M12)  ▶ Eco-Low-Fire v1 → pilot at Vista Alegre / Costa Verde
 H3 (M9–M18)  ▶ Digital color matching live; Eco-Low-Fire v2 (broader bodies)
 H4 (M12–M24) ▶ AI Glaze Prediction Platform beta → key-account rollout
```

> **Innovation principle:** ship the **energy win first** (immediate, measurable, bankable), use it to earn trust and data, then layer the **AI platform** to make the relationship structurally sticky.

## 6. Go-to-Market for Key Accounts

### 6.1 Account-based, not volume-based

This is a **named-account** game (a few dozen factories), not mass marketing. Tier the targets:

| Tier | Accounts | Role | Entry wedge |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — Lighthouse** | Vista Alegre, Costa Verde | Reference + co-development credibility | Eco-Low-Fire pilot on one product line |
| **Tier 2 — Volume** | Grestel, Matceramica, Spal | Revenue base | Consistency SLA + energy savings |
| **Tier 3 — Expansion** | Sanitaryware & tiles (Sanindusa, Revigrés, Love Tiles…) | Scale-out | Engobes/glazes + local service |

### 6.2 The "land" play — how to enter a key account

1. **Diagnostic visit (free):** engineer audits one line — firing curve, defect rate, energy/piece, current glaze cost. Deliver a **written opportunity report** (this alone differentiates us).
2. **Single-line pilot:** Eco-Low-Fire or a consistency-locked custom frit on **one product / one kiln** — low risk for the customer.
3. **Quantified result:** measured €/year energy saving + defect reduction, signed off jointly.
4. **Consistency SLA contract:** convert the proof into a multi-line supply agreement with the ΔE / batch guarantee.
5. **Expand:** roll into adjacent lines, then onboard them to the AI platform → lock-in.

### 6.3 Commercial model

- **Value-based pricing** anchored on **TCO and energy €-savings**, not €/kg.
- **Free technical service bundled** (funded by the §4 cost savings) — turns service into a moat instead of a cost.
- **Joint-development agreements** with Tier-1 accounts (shared IP on application know-how, exclusivity windows on co-developed effects).

### 6.4 Proof assets to build (sales enablement)

- Energy-saving **case study one-pagers** (€/year, before/after).
- **Consistency dashboard** the customer can log into (ΔE trend, CoA history).
- **Sustainability sheet** (recycled cullet %, CO₂/kg, lead-free status) for the customer's own ESG reporting.

## 7. Pilot Projects

> Pilots are the entire go-to-market engine. Two lighthouse pilots, designed to produce **undeniable, quantified proof.**

### 7.1 Pilot A — Vista Alegre (premium porcelain)

- **Objective:** prove Eco-Low-Fire on a high-whiteness porcelain line **without any loss of whiteness or gloss.**
- **Scope:** one product family, one kiln; 50–200 kg pilot batches.
- **Success metrics:**
  - **−30 to −50 °C** peak firing temp,
  - **≥ 8%** measured kiln energy reduction,
  - **whiteness/gloss within reference tolerance**,
  - **ΔE ≤ 0.5** batch-to-batch over 3 consecutive lots.
- **Why Vista Alegre first:** the toughest quality bar in Portugal. Winning here is a **quality certificate** that de-risks every subsequent account.

### 7.2 Pilot B — Costa Verde (high-volume quality tableware)

- **Objective:** prove **batch consistency SLA + 24–48h onsite service** on a volume line, plus energy saving.
- **Scope:** a glossy/white glaze on a continuous line.
- **Success metrics:**
  - defect/firing-reject rate **↓ (target −20%)**,
  - **energy/piece ↓ 8–12%**,
  - **field-engineer response time ≤ 48h** logged on every incident,
  - documented **€/year saving** signed off by their process team.
- **Why Costa Verde:** demonstrates the *operational/service* moat (proximity, SLA) on a cost-sensitive volume line — complementary proof to Vista Alegre's *quality* proof.

### 7.3 Pilot governance

- Joint steering team (Vidres applications lead + customer process owner), **bi-weekly review**.
- Single shared **scorecard** (energy, ΔE, defects, response time, €-impact).
- Pre-agreed **conversion clause**: hitting metrics → multi-line supply agreement.
